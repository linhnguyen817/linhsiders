crappy travel photos

[planning]
i am not a professional photographer, nor do i aspire to be one.

at the beginning of my trip, i decided i would just show my friends and family the already-plentiful 4K, color graded travel photos on instagram for a better visual representation of the places i visited.

with the energy and time i saved not trying to get the perfect shot, i would look for and note the smaller, quirkier things i discovered in these cities.

so many things!

do it for the 'gram

[design process]
instagram stories disappear after 24 hours and with the overwhelming amount of them every day, every post i shared to my followers needed to be visually interesting, thoughtful, and concise.

i chose to release each post in this series on a separate day, consecutively over the course of 10 days.

for actually designing and editing each post, i debated between using just the basic instagram story editor with its limited interface and features or going to a third-party editor with all its fancy tools.

i ultimately chose to creating everything in the instagram app for the most authentic look and feel.

every post had both english and spanish translations and took about an hour each to write, design, and do at least two iterations because the instagram app loved to crash!

adjusting tiny details via pinching on my iPhone's 4.7‚Äù display was a daily test of perseverance

however, being constrained by the medium and the four tools (text, draw, highlight, and stickers) in the instagram story editor turned out to be a fun challenge. it pushed me to be more creative with the writing, composition, and typography of each post.
